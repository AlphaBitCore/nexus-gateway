# Wiki expansion — cleanup candidates

> **Purpose**: subagents writing wiki pages may surface obsolete code, dead
> documentation, unused scripts, or stale memory anchors during their research.
> Rather than deleting in-band (risky — subagent has narrow context), each
> finding is appended here. Opus reviews centrally, runs strict verification,
> and either deletes (logging the decision in `_decisions.md` as
> `DEC-CLEANUP-NNN`) or marks the finding "keep" with a one-line rationale.
>
> **Strict verification gate** for any deletion:
> 1. `git grep` across the repo confirms no other file references the
>    symbol/path/script.
> 2. `git log -- <file>` shows the history — if it was added recently with no
>    rationale, lean toward keep + ask.
> 3. Check other worktrees (`git worktree list`) for in-flight branches that
>    may rely on it.
> 4. If the candidate is a public-facing artifact (script in `scripts/`, doc
>    that is linked from outside the repo, OpenAPI spec), require explicit
>    user approval before delete.
> 5. Cleanups land in a SEPARATE commit from wiki content (commit hygiene —
>    see CLAUDE.md and the 2026-05-21 bundled-commit incident).

## How subagents append

Subagents append rows to the table below. Format:

```
| YYYY-MM-DD | <subagent / batch> | <path or symbol> | <kind: code/doc/script/memory> | <why subagent suspects dead> | <pending verification by Opus> |
```

Subagents do NOT delete anything. They append and move on.
